\section{Relation to earlier work}\label{sec:related-work}

We use \emph{finite-order} Polya-frequency terminology in the sense reviewed
by Belton, Guillot, Khare, and Putinar~\cite{belton}. Khare's characterization
of measurable \(\TN_p\) functions for \(p\geq3\) gives a broader finite-order
context for translation-kernel criteria~\cite{khare}; the present proof instead
derives a kernel-specific strict order-four criterion through quotient
Wronskians and a curvature transport identity.

For the same normalization of the Riemann kernel, Dimitrov and Xu relate
Wronskians of Fourier and Laplace transforms to correlation kernels and
real-zero criteria~\cite{dimitrov-xu}. Csordas and Varga obtain strict
concavity and moment inequalities for the Riemann kernel in connection with
the Riemann Hypothesis~\cite{csordas-varga}. Those works concern adjacent
Wronskian, moment, and Fourier questions. Our theorem here is the finite
translation-minor statement in Theorem~\ref{thm:main}; no comprehensive
priority claim beyond that precise proved statement is made.
