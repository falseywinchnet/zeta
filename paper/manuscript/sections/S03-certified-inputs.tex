\section{Certified one-variable inputs}\label{sec:inputs}

Define
\[
 F_1=qq''-(q')^2,\qquad F_2=q^3-F_1.
\]
For the logarithmic cumulants \(c_j=\ell^{(j)}\), set
\[
\begin{aligned}
 &m_0=1,\quad m_1=0,\quad m_2=c_2,\quad m_3=c_3,\\
 &m_4=c_4+3c_2^2,\quad m_5=c_5+10c_2c_3,\\
 &m_6=c_6+15c_2c_4+10c_3^2+15c_2^3,
\end{aligned}
\]
and
\begin{equation}\label{eq:C4-def}
 \Cfour(t)=\det[m_{i+j-2}(t)]_{i,j=1}^4.
\end{equation}

\begin{lemma}[Certified input]\label{lem:certified-input}
For the kernel \(\Phi\),
\[
 q(t)>0,\qquad F_2(t)>0,\qquad \Cfour(t)>0
 \quad(t\in\R).
\]
\end{lemma}

The compact interval \([0,1]\) is covered with directed Arb balls at 192-bit
precision. The retained lower bounds are
\[
 q\geq18.7268,\qquad F_2\geq3889.2,
 \qquad \Cfour\geq2.817\times10^7.
\]
The first certificate uses 7731 adaptive second-order Taylor cells and the
second 8050. Evenness covers \([-1,1]\).

For \(t\geq1\), put \(x=\pi e^{2t}\). The cumulants have directed enclosures
\[
 c_j=-2^jx+\varepsilon_j,\qquad
 |\varepsilon_j|\leq\frac{E_j}{x},\qquad 2\leq j\leq6,
\]
where
\[
 (E_2,E_3,E_4,E_5,E_6)=(19.8,176,2082,30770,545900).
\]
They imply \(q,F_2>0\) on the tail. Substitution in the determinant
\eqref{eq:C4-def} gives
\[
 \frac{\Cfour(t)}{x^6}>44392\qquad(x\geq\pi e^2).
\]
The derivation of the \(E_j\), the exact lower polynomial, and its monotonic
normalized margin are recorded in \appref{app:tail}. These are the only
directed numerical inputs to the main positive proof.
