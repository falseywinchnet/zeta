The global-sign verifier retains two theta modes exactly, checks 361 rational
Bernstein or decay coefficients and 140 derivative-envelope coefficients,
and bounds all modes \(n\geq3\) by the analytic estimates in
\appref{app:tail}. Its launcher forces SymPy's pure-Python rational domain and
rejects every attempted FLINT import. There is no interval subdivision and no
floating-point sign decision.

The origin order-five certificate is independent. It uses only
standard-library integer arithmetic with outward rounding on a
\(10^{-36}\)-rational grid, Machin's formula, alternating Taylor sums, three
retained theta modes, and one geometric tail bound. The separator certificate
reconstructs all 73 positive coefficients of its order-four numerator and
checks the discriminant inequality in \appref{app:separator} over the exact
rationals.

The quotient, curvature, primitive, endpoint, density-ratio, and
confluent-limit statements are mathematical identities for generic smooth
functions. The paper displays their derivations; the symbolic scripts replay
the algebra. High-precision values at \(t=1/3,1,4\) and several Riemann-kernel
points are regression checks only and are not premises of a sign conclusion.

The former 192-bit Arb covers of 7731 and 8050 cells are retained in the
repository as historical certificates, but they are archived and are not
dependencies of this paper or of routine replay. The collision, Hermite,
escape, and spatial-atlas computations are likewise outside the proof graph.

A focused independent audit should proceed in this order:
\begin{enumerate}
 \item verify the analytic theta normalization in \eqref{eq:kernel-series};
 \item check \eqref{eq:raw-signs}, the two-mode Bernstein domains, and the
       later-mode estimates in \appref{app:tail};
 \item check the two weighted-mean splits in \secref{sec:pf3};
 \item expand the determinant and curvature identity in
       \appref{app:algebra};
 \item check the endpoint identity in \appref{app:endpoints};
 \item differentiate the density ratio in
       \eqref{eq:density-ratio-derivative};
 \item derive the parity factorization and divided-difference limit in
       \secref{sec:completion}, then replay the rational origin certificate;
 \item reconstruct the separator determinant and exact discriminant proof in
       \appref{app:separator}.
\end{enumerate}
No numerical scan is used as evidence for global nonconfluent positivity of
either kernel.

The non-mutating entry point \path{scripts/replay_paper.py} checks all five
registered verifier outputs. Exact package versions are in
\path{requirements-paper.txt}; python-flint is not a paper dependency.
