# Boundaries and failed interpretations

- A negative confluent coefficient for all sufficiently small `u` does not by
  itself identify the first finite-spacing failure. The finite negative region
  extends to a larger center.
- The root of `C5` and the most negative finite determinant are not the same
  extremum. "Largest" must mean outermost center if it is to coincide with
  "earliest intercept."
- A grid search cannot prove global outermostness. The broad scan is retained
  only to identify the tangency and possible competing components.
- The two-mode determinant is not accurate enough for the requested threshold:
  its root is displaced by about `8.7e-5`. Three modes reduce the displacement
  to about `9.3e-13`.
