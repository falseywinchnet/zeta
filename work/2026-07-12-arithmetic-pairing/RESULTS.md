# Results

No dominance proof was obtained from the proposed pairing.

What is determined:

1. Suzuki already proved the one-crossing kernel theorem and proved that the
   requested eventual dominance for all `0<omega<1/2` is equivalent to RH.
2. Every finite-prime-chain packet is asymptotically negative.
3. Every bounded-to-one allocation lacks the required
   `(x/n)^(1-2omega)` resource count.
4. Leading average order cancels exactly and cannot decide the sign.
5. Local Euler factors fail PF2, so standard variation diminution is absent.
6. Divisibility-based max-flow allocations fail; adding a global prime pool
   does not remain sufficient as the scale grows.
7. A successful pairing requires new sieve-level Hall inequalities controlling
   the full generalized Jordan-totient discrepancy.

The dominance statement remains a valid exact RH criterion. Positivity of the
coefficients and the one-crossing kernel do not supply its proof.
