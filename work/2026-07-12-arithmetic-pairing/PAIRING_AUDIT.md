# Pairing audit

## Exact status from Suzuki

Suzuki's 2012 monotonicity paper uses the same explicit kernel and arithmetic
coefficients. It proves:

- the kernel has exactly one zero `y_omega` in `(0,1)` and is positive above it;
- eventual nonnegativity of the weighted sum for every
  `0<omega<1/2` is equivalent to RH.

Thus the dominance inequality is not an unproved auxiliary lemma surrounding
RH. It is an exact RH criterion.

## Dyadic pairing

Write each integer as `2^k m` with `m` odd. Supermultiplicativity is exact along
the chain:

`c_omega(2^k m)=2^(k omega)(1-2^(-2omega))c_omega(m)` for `k>=1`.

The resulting kernel packet is

`D_omega(y)=G_omega(y)`

`+(1-2^(-2omega))sum_{1<=k<=log_2(1/y)}2^(komega)G_omega(2^k y)`.

It is negative for small `y`. Indeed,

`G_omega(y)~-C_omega y^(omega-1)`,

while the total contribution of the bounded positive lobe is at most

`O(y^(-omega))`.

Since `omega-1<-omega` for `omega<1/2`, the negative term dominates.

## Every finite-prime packet fails

Let `S` be the multiplicative semigroup generated by finitely many primes, and
allow any packet weights bounded by `O(m^omega)` on `m in S`. The number of
semigroup elements below `Y` is `O((log Y)^r)`, where `r` is the number of
generators. Hence its positive-lobe contribution is at most

`O(y^(-omega)(log(1/y))^r)`.

The unpaired base term remains `-C_omega y^(omega-1)`. Their ratio tends to zero
because `1-2omega>0`. Therefore every finite collection of prime-power chains
fails.

Any successful allocation must use a set of ordinary multiples whose size
grows polynomially with `x/n`. The complete Euler product is essential.

More precisely, put `M=x/n`. The negative demand of a fixed small index has
size comparable to `c_omega(n)M^(1-omega)`. For every multiplier `m<=M`,

`c_omega(nm)/c_omega(n)<=m^omega<=M^omega`,

and the positive kernel lobe is bounded. An exclusive allocation therefore
needs at least order `M^(1-2omega)` high multiples for that low index. Any
bounded-to-one pairing fails for every `omega<1/2`.

## Average order cannot decide the sign

The main density of the coefficients is

`t^omega dt/zeta(1+2omega)`.

Its pairing against the kernel is exactly zero. Therefore upper and lower
bounds that retain only the leading average order give equality at main scale.
The sign belongs to the second-order generalized Jordan-totient discrepancy.

This explains why interval-mass arguments appear to have ample positive
resources before resource sharing, yet lose all slack after the full allocation
is counted once.
