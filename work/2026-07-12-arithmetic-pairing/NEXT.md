# Next decision

Do not continue adding ad hoc pairing edges. Each enlargement either fails at a
larger scale or converges to the original dominance inequality.

A renewed pairing attempt requires one genuinely new theorem of one of these
forms:

1. a collective Euler-product sign-transfer theorem not inherited from local
   factors;
2. a canonical factorization of every integer giving provable Hall
   neighborhoods;
3. a one-sided generalized Jordan-totient discrepancy bound whose hypotheses
   are independently provable without a zero-free assumption.

Absent such a theorem, return to the other deterministic forward requirement:
a positive factorization or comparison identity for the complete Weil or causal
operator. Do not substitute average sign, finite sampling, or local continuity.
