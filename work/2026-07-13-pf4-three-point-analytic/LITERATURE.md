## Csordas and Varga (1988)

George Csordas and Richard S. Varga, *Moment Inequalities and the Riemann
Hypothesis*, Constructive Approximation 4 (1988), 175-198.

- Local evidence already present:
  `papers/csordas-varga-moment-inequalities.pdf`.
- Author-hosted locator:
  https://www.math.kent.edu/~varga/pub/paper_161.pdf
- Relevant statement: Theorem 2.1 proves strict concavity of
  `log(Phi(sqrt(t)))` for `t>0`.
- In curvature notation this gives, after the paper/repository scaling is
  aligned, the radial inequality `u q(u)+s(u)>0` for `u>0`, equivalently the
  endpoint value of `q` exceeds its average from the origin.
- Relevant method: Section 3 isolates the first theta mode, establishes signs
  of explicit derivative polynomials, and bounds the remaining theta modes by
  geometric tails.  This is a useful architecture for analytic tail and core
  lemmas.
- Limitation: the result controls a lower-order radial mean.  It does not
  imply the three-point rate inequality `K>=0` and contains no PF4 claim.

The PDF is a rotated scan.  Pages 178 and 185-194 were rendered and visually
inspected; the proof is not being inferred from search-engine snippets.

## Finite-order total positivity and Wronskians

Steven N. Karp, *Wronskians, total positivity, and real Schubert calculus*,
arXiv:2110.02301.

- Primary locator: https://arxiv.org/abs/2110.02301
- Relevant theme: nonvanishing initial Wronskians characterize Markov/ECT
  systems in the finite-dimensional polynomial-flag setting.
- Limitation: this does not supply a sign theorem for the Riemann translate
  family or a local-jet criterion implying the required nonlocal Wronskian.
  The repository's certified quotient/iterated-integral proof already supplies
  the ECT-to-collocation step needed here.

Alexander Belton, Dominique Guillot, Apoorva Khare, and Mihai Putinar,
*Preservers of Totally Positive Kernels and Pólya Frequency Functions*,
arXiv:2110.08206.

- Local evidence already present: `papers/belton-total-positivity.pdf`.
- Primary locator: https://arxiv.org/abs/2110.08206
- Relevant scope: definitions, preservers, and Schoenberg's PF-infinity
  transform characterization.
- Limitation: the PF-infinity transform theorem does not characterize a fixed
  finite order such as PF4 and does not settle the three-point inequality.
