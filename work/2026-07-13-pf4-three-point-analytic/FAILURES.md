# Failed or insufficient routes

## Treating C4 as the whole theorem

The exact collision formula shows why `C4>0` matters, but also where it stops:
it proves the point where both gaps vanish.  The open object still contains a
nonlocal two-interval rate comparison.  No silent upgrade from confluent PF4
to global PF4 is permitted.

## Direct Taylor expansion of the original rational formula

A generic CAS expansion of `partial_x Psi` spent more than ninety seconds in
removable singularities and was interrupted.  Expanding the positive objects
`lambda`, `H`, and the coefficients of `K` separately reduces the calculation
to a short exact identity and exposes `-q C4/(3 C3^2)`.  The failed approach is
also analytically inferior because it hides the positive factors.

## Lower-order radial concavity

Csordas--Varga's strict concavity of `log(Phi(sqrt(t)))` is genuine and useful,
but it controls only an endpoint-versus-average inequality for `q`.  The PF4
target involves translated rates of two different positive Peano means.  The
literature theorem is input for estimates, not a solution.

## Raw tail dominance in the rational formula

Substituting `q approximately 4 pi exp(2t)` suggests the correct sign, but
denominators `A(x,m)` and `Lambda` become small as gaps collide.  A relative
error estimate on the rational formula is therefore not uniform.  The exact
exponential calculation remains useful only after passing to the
division-free numerator `G` and separating small-gap Taylor control.

## Absolute monotonicity of q on the positive half-line

A tempting simplification is to hope that every derivative of `q` is positive
for `u>0`, making all interval comparisons automatic.  Isolated directed-jet
evaluations were used only to test this premise: `q^(4)(0)` is negative and
`q^(3)(0.25)` is negative, while later values change sign.  Thus absolute
monotonicity is false and should not be built into a proof.  This was a
hypothesis test, not a parameter sweep and not evidence for the target sign.

## Numerical sweeps

No new sweep was run.  Sampling cannot distinguish a true positive Peano
density from a thin nonlocal sign failure and cannot certify the theorem.
