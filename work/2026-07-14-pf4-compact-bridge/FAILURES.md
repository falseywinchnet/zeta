# Failed route

The proposed adaptive anchor extension began with 34,272 new continuum boxes.
It was stopped during the first wave.  No manifest was written and no result
was inferred from the unfinished calculation.

The defect is conceptual: although a positive interval box proves its whole
continuum, the refinement tree had no a priori termination or complexity
bound.  Subdivision was compensating for dependency loss in the evaluator.
That is not an analytical completion strategy and could continue indefinitely
if the margin vanishes, the coordinates remain ill-conditioned, or the target
is false.

The scalar Peano and node-Taylor experiments are retained only as diagnostics
showing where algebraic cancellation occurs.  They are not evidence for a
global sign.

The curvature-coordinate reduction has not yet proved that local `C4>0`
integrates to the nonlocal numerator.  No theorem found in the targeted
Wronskian/disconjugacy literature applies this implication directly to the
translation-kernel flow.
