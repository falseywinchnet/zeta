# Curvature-coordinate closed form

This note replaces the abandoned spatial atlas by one global analytic
coordinate.  Put

\[
y(t)=-s(t),\qquad \frac{dy}{dt}=q(t)>0,
\qquad Q(y(t))=q(t).
\]

Thus `y` is a global increasing coordinate.  Derivatives below are with
respect to `y`.  The identities

\[
Q'=\frac{q'}q=f,
\qquad Q''=\frac{f'}q,
\qquad \rho:=1-Q''=\frac hq>0
\]

follow directly.  Define

\[
\kappa:=2-Q''=1+\rho=1+\frac hq.
\]

The existing global bounds `q>=18` and `0<f'<8` give

\[
\frac{14}{9}<\kappa\le2,
\qquad 0\le Q''<\frac49.
\]

## The three-point quantity is one concave divided difference

For `p=y(x)<z=y(m)<r=y(r)` write `L=z-p` and `R=r-z`.  Since

\[
M(u,v)=Q[y(u),y(v)],
\]

the exact PF3 quantity becomes

\[
\Lambda=L+R+Q[p,z]-Q[z,r].
\]

If `g(y)=Q(y)-y^2`, then

\[
\boxed{\Lambda=g[p,z]-g[z,r]=-(r-p)g[p,z,r].}
\]

Moreover `-g''=\kappa>0`.  The Peano formula is therefore

\[
\boxed{
\Lambda=
L\int_0^1u\,\kappa(p+uL)\,du
+R\int_0^1(1-v)\,\kappa(z+vR)\,dv .}
\tag{1}
\]

The normalized left derivative is

\[
\delta:=\frac H{Q(p)}=-\partial_p\Lambda
=\boxed{\int_0^1(1-u)\kappa(p+uL)\,du}.                 \tag{2}
\]

Thus both positive objects in the PF4 quotient are triangular integrals of
the same globally positive function.  In particular

\[
\frac79\le\delta\le1,
\qquad \frac79(L+R)\le\Lambda\le L+R.
\]

## Translation is the flow generated by Q

Simultaneous translation in the original coordinate becomes

\[
\mathcal T=Q(p)\partial_p+Q(z)\partial_z+Q(r)\partial_r.
\]

For an interval `[a,b]`, let

\[
V_{ab}(u)=(1-u)Q(a)+uQ(b),
\qquad
U(a,b)=\int_0^1V_{ab}(u)\kappa(a+u(b-a))\,du .          \tag{3}
\]

Differentiating (1)--(2) under the integral and integrating every
`\kappa'` term by parts gives the closed identities

\[
\boxed{\mathcal T\Lambda=U(z,r)-U(p,z),}                \tag{4}
\]

\[
\boxed{
\mathcal T\delta=
\frac{U(p,z)-[Q(z)-Q(p)]\delta-Q(p)\kappa(p)}L .}       \tag{5}
\]

No Taylor expansion, grid, or limiting argument is used.  The transport
moment also has the endpoint form

\[
U(a,b)=Q(a)+Q(b)
-\frac{Q(b)Q'(b)-Q(a)Q'(a)}{b-a}
+\frac{[Q(b)-Q(a)]^2}{(b-a)^2}.                         \tag{6}
\]

## Closed remaining inequality

Since `H=Q(p)\delta`, the PF4 numerator divided by the already-positive
factor `B` is

\[
\mathcal N=
\delta\Lambda^2
+\Lambda\{Q'(p)\delta+\mathcal T\delta\}
-\delta\mathcal T\Lambda .                             \tag{7}
\]

Using (4)--(5), this is equivalently

\[
\boxed{
\begin{aligned}
\mathcal N={}&
\delta\Lambda\{\Lambda+Q'(p)-Q[p,z]\}
-\frac{\Lambda Q(p)\kappa(p)}L\\
&+\left(\delta+\frac\Lambda L\right)U(p,z)
-\delta U(z,r).
\end{aligned}}
\tag{8}
\]

Global PF4 is exactly `\mathcal N>=0` for every `p<z<r`.  Formula (8) is the
closed domain problem: it contains only two one-dimensional integrals of one
positive curvature function.  There is no outer band or spatial atlas.

## The local order-four invariant collapses too

The confluent invariants simplify dramatically:

\[
C_3=Q^3\kappa,
\]

\[
\boxed{
C_4=Q^6\kappa^2
\left[3(\kappa-1)-\{Q(\log\kappa)'\}'\right].}
\tag{9}
\]

Hence the already certified `C4>0` is precisely the local differential
inequality

\[
\boxed{\{Q(\log\kappa)'\}'<3(\kappa-1).}                \tag{10}
\]

For constant `Q''=c<1` (equivalently constant `\kappa=2-c`), exact
substitution in (7) gives

\[
\mathcal N=
\frac{(2-c)^2(1-c)}4(L+R)^2>0.                          \tag{11}
\]

Thus the entire nonlocal obstruction is variation of `\kappa`; the constant
curvature model is proved by the PF3 inequality `1-Q''>0` alone.  The next
analytical question is now exact and one-dimensional: determine whether
(10), together with `14/9<\kappa<=2` and `0<=Q''<4/9`, integrates through
(1)--(8) to force `\mathcal N>=0`, or identify the additional nonlocal term
that survives.
