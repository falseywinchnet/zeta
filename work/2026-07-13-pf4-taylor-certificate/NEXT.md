# Remaining theorem package

1. Extract an explicit collision-divided lower bound from the sparse
   positive-coefficient dominant-theta `J_b` and `S_r` polynomials.
2. Bound the full-theta perturbation after Vandermonde division, so the error
   is compared with a nonvanishing normalized margin rather than raw `J`.
3. Join that estimate to the `C4`, fourth-coefficient, and `N6` collision
   expansion at an explicit gap threshold, uniformly for both tails.
4. Certify the `theta=0` function `E/rho^2` and the distinct 21-term
   `theta=1` function on their compact residues, with their own confluent and
   tail modules.
5. Once these lemmas supply an actual bounded residue, run the degree-five
   Taylor cover adaptively and emit every cell-domain hash and lower bound.

These are not optional optimizations.  They are the unresolved mathematical
premises of obligations 5 and 6.
