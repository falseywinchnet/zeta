# Later research directions

## Highest-value extraction

Try to turn the explicit three-variable Keller map into a small explicit failure
of one equivalent or sufficient closure statement:

1. normalize the determinant to one;
2. trace the map through the Special Image Conjecture reduction;
3. then through the complex-Gaussian realization used by
   Derksen--van den Essen--Zhao;
4. record the exact dimension increase and identify explicit polynomials `P,Q`;
5. verify `E(P^m)=0` for all `m>=1` and infinitely many nonzero
   `E(P^m Q)` without relying on the global contrapositive alone.

This would make the Gaussian Moments failure concrete.  It should remain a
separate algebraic research program unless a direct reciprocal-kernel mapping is
found.
