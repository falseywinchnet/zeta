# Frame S09 — non-vacuous crossing kernel

Source: `paper/manuscript/sections/S09-crossing-kernel.tex`, CERT9.

## Exact proposition

For the actual probability measures constructed in S08, if
`Δ=Fμ-Fν`, then `Δ(p)=Δ(w)=0` and `Δ(y)>0` for every `p<y<w`.

## Expanded because

1. `Fμ(p)=Fν(p)=0` and `Fμ(w)=Fν(w)=1` **because** both measures have total
   mass one and the stated compact supports (PO-0030/0031).
2. On `(z,w)`, `Fμ=1` **because** `μ` is supported on `[p,z]`.
3. `ν((y,w])>0` for `z≤y<w` **because** its right density is strictly
   positive on the nonempty interval `(y,w)`; hence `Δ(y)>0` there.
4. On `(p,z)`, both densities are strictly positive and their *actual ratio*
   simplifies to `Λ(z-y)/(Lδ(y-p))` (PO-0033).
5. The ratio derivative is negative **because** `Λ,L,δ,z-p,(y-p)²` are all
   derived positive—not because `C` was declared positive.
6. The ratio tends to `+∞` at `p+` and `0` at `z-` **because** the numerator
   and denominator have the corresponding one-sided limits with nonzero
   remaining factors.
7. A ratio-one point exists **because** explicit near-endpoint values lie on
   opposite sides of one and continuity applies on a closed subinterval.
8. It is unique **because** the ratio is strictly decreasing.
9. `Δ'` is positive before and negative after the crossing **because** the
   `ν` density is positive and `fμ-fν=fν(R₀-1)`.
10. `Δ(z)=ν((z,w])>0` by Step 3, not by an assumed symbol.
11. `Δ>0` before the crossing **because** it increases strictly from zero.
12. `Δ>0` after the crossing through `z` **because** it decreases toward the
    already positive value `Δ(z)`.
13. Steps 3 and 12 cover all of `(p,w)`.

## Counterexample interfaces

- a zero or negative density normalizer;
- failure of either total-mass identity;
- a second ratio-one point;
- zero right mass despite `z<w`;
- a point in `(p,w)` where `Δ≤0`.

The former CAS assertions exclude none of these by themselves.
