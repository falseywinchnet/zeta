# Frame S03 — certified one-variable inputs

Source: `paper/manuscript/sections/S03-certified-inputs.tex`, Appendix A2,
CERT12.

## Proposition

For the concrete kernel `Φ`, `q(t)>0`, `F₂(t)>0`, and `C₄(t)>0` for every
real `t`.

## Expanded because

1. The invariants are rational polynomials in a normalized derivative jet
   **because** differentiation and denominator clearing give exact identities.
2. The first two theta modes exceed explicit margins on fixed algebraic
   domains **because** every relevant rational Bernstein coefficient satisfies
   its certified sign.
3. All later modes fit explicit derivative envelopes **because** their
   polynomial factors have coefficient bounds and their exponentials form a
   controlled geometric tail.
4. Tail perturbations are smaller than the two-mode margins **because** the
   exact rational inequalities checked by CERT12 compare cleared numerators on
   both `π≤x≤5` and `x≥5`.
5. The positive half-line covers `t≥0` **because** `x=πe^{2t}` is increasing
   from `π` to infinity.
6. The negative half-line follows **because** the required invariants have the
   parity induced by even `Φ` and the sign-bearing ones are even.

## Certificate boundary

CERT12 checks exact symbolic/rational algebra. A formal bridge must still prove
that its parsed polynomials equal PO-0011–PO-0013 for T0's `Φ`, that all cleared
denominators are positive, and that every domain is covered.

## Counterexample condition

Any real `t` with one invariant nonpositive. A finite search cannot exclude
this; only the certified domain reduction and analytic tail can.
