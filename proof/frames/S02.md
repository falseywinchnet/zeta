# Frame S02 — kernel and smooth even continuation

Source: `paper/manuscript/sections/S02-kernel.tex`

## Proposition bundle

The theta construction defines an analytic even real function `H`; the kernel
`Φ=(H''-H/4)/2` equals the displayed theta-mode series, is even, and is
strictly positive on `ℝ`. Consequently `log Φ` is globally defined.

## Expanded because

1. `ϑ(x)` exists for `x>0` **because** its Gaussian terms admit a summable
   geometric comparison away from `x=0` (PO-0001).
2. Differentiated substituted series converge locally uniformly **because**
   every compact `t`-interval gives a common polynomial-times-Gaussian
   majorant (PO-0002).
3. `H(-t)=H(t)` **because** the theta transformation at `e^{2t}` gives
   `ϑ(e^{-2t})=e^tϑ(e^{2t})` (PO-0003/0004).
4. The mode series for `Φ` **because** PO-0002 permits two termwise
   differentiations and elementary simplification (PO-0005).
5. Every positive-side term is positive **because**
   `2πn²e^{2t}-3 ≥ 2π-3 > 0` for `n≥1,t≥0`.
6. The infinite sum is strictly positive **because** it contains positive
   terms and at least the `n=1` term (PO-0008), not merely because all terms are
   nonnegative.
7. Global positivity **because** `Φ` is even (PO-0007).

## Non-vacuity witness

The series constructs a concrete function before any positivity subtype or
logarithm is introduced. At `t=0` the first mode is a concrete positive term,
so the domain is inhabited and the positivity statement is not empty.
