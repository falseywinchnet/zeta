## Proposition bundle

The explicit densities define probability measures `μ,ν`, and
`K=Eν[A₀]-Eμ[A₀]`.

## Expanded because

1. The `μ` density is nonnegative and integrable **because** `κ>0`, `z-y≥0`
   on `[p,z]`, and all normalizers are positive.
2. Its total mass is one **because** the numerator integral equals `L²δ` by
   the triangular definition of `δ`.
3. Both `ν` pieces are nonnegative and integrable by the same endpoint signs.
4. Their combined mass is one **because** the numerator integrals sum to
   `LΛ` after matching the two triangular terms exactly.
5. `A₀'=D` and the `H,J` primitive identities **because** direct
   differentiation gives equal functions, not merely equal samples.
6. Weighted integration by parts gives the expectation endpoint expression
   **because** all functions are regular on compact intervals and the boundary
   terms are retained.
7. Differentiating triangular integrals along `T` gives the two `U` formulas
   **because** endpoint and integrand motion are both included.
8. The two endpoint expressions agree **because** clearing only already-proved
   nonzero denominators reduces their difference to the zero polynomial.
