# Frame S04 — order three and positive slope functional

Source: `paper/manuscript/sections/S04-pf3.tex`, CERT28.

## Proposition

If `q>0` and `F₂>0` globally, then `Λ(ξ;m,r)>0` for every `ξ<m<r`; this
supplies the strict order-three Wronskian factor.

## Expanded because

1. `A(a,b)=∫_a^b q` **because** `s'=-q` and the fundamental theorem.
2. `M(a,b)` is the `q`-weighted mean of `q'/q` **because** its numerator and
   denominator are the integrals of `q'` and `q`.
3. Compact-interval extrema exist **because** `q'/q` is continuous and the
   intervals are compact and nonempty.
4. Weighted means lie between extrema **because** the weight `q` is strictly
   positive and has positive total mass.
5. The difference of selected endpoint values is bounded by
   `∫ max((q'/q)',0)` **because** negative derivative portions can only reduce
   net increase.
6. `(q'/q)'=F₁/q²` by quotient differentiation.
7. `q³-max(F₁,0)=min(q³,F₂)` by cases on the sign of `F₁`.
8. The final integral is strict **because** `min(q³,F₂)/q²` is continuous and
   positive on the positive-length interval `(ξ,r)`.

## Non-vacuity witness

`ξ<m<r` constructs two nonempty compact intervals; their `q`-mass is positive.
No weighted probability measure is invoked before this mass fact.
