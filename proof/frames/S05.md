# Frame S05 — quotient/Wronskian reduction

Source: `paper/manuscript/sections/S05-quotient-reduction.tex`, CERT5.

## Proposition bundle

For a sufficiently smooth positive kernel with strict lower-order factors,
successive quotient differentiation reduces strict order-four translation
minors to strict decrease of `Ψ(·;m,r)`. Conversely global PF4 forces weak
decrease.

## Expanded because

1. `v_j>0` **because** direct quotient differentiation factors it as
   `(u_j/u_1)A(p_j,p_1)`, with both factors positive.
2. Wronskian factorizations **because** successive column division and
   derivative row operations preserve the displayed positive prefactors.
3. `g_j` equals the stated `Λ` ratio **because** the weighted-mean split is an
   exact partition of the interval at `p₂`.
4. `w_j>0` **because** `v_j/v₂>0`, the `A` ratio is positive, and `Λ>0`.
5. The logarithmic derivative of `w₄/w₃` equals a difference of `Ψ` values
   **because** exact common terms cancel with the audited sign convention.
6. A positive final derivative gives every finite minor **because** repeated
   fundamental-theorem row differences yield an iterated integral over
   positive-length ordered boxes (PO-0018).
7. Global PF4 gives weak Wronskian signs **because** one-sided confluent limits
   divide only by positive Vandermonde factors (PO-0019).

## Strictness edge

It is insufficient to prove only a confluent Wronskian positive. The repeated
integral must have a strictly positive integrand on a nonempty domain for every
strictly ordered pair of node tuples.

Status: conventional/certificate supported, not formal.
