# Frame S07 — confluent invariant in curvature form

Source: `paper/manuscript/sections/S07-confluent-invariant.tex`, Appendix A1,
CERT9 and CERT12.

## Proposition

`C₄ = Q⁶κ²D`, where `D=3(κ-1)-{Q(log κ)'}'`; consequently `D>0`.

## Expanded because

1. `d/dt = Q d/dy` **because** `dy/dt=Q(y(t))`.
2. `c₂=-Q` **because** `c₂=ℓ''=-q` and `Q∘y=q`.
3. Higher cumulants follow by repeated application of the same differential
   operator; each recurrence is an equality obligation.
4. The central-moment determinant equals its Schur-complement expression
   **because** determinant-preserving row/column algebra is exact.
5. Substitution factors the determinant as `Q⁶κ²D` **because** the cleared
   expanded numerator is the zero polynomial.
6. `D>0` **because** `C₄>0`, `Q⁶>0`, and `κ²>0`; division is legitimate.

## Object identity

The determinant definition of `C₄` is primary. The thirteen-term polynomial
and curvature expression must both be proved equal to that determinant before
their certificates can support T1.
