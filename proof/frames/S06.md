# Frame S06 — curvature coordinate and sign bridge

Source: `paper/manuscript/sections/S06-curvature-coordinate.tex`, CERT9,
CERT19, CERT26.

## Proposition bundle

The strictly increasing coordinate `y=-s` converts the three-point quantities
to endpoint functions `Q,p,z,w`; it yields positive triangular forms for
`Λ,δ` and the exact identity `∂ξΨ=-Q(p)N/Λ²`.

## Expanded because

1. `dy/dt=q(t)>0` **because** `y=-s` and `q=-s'`.
2. `Q(y(t))=q(t)` is well-defined on the image **because** strict monotonicity
   makes `y` injective; no global surjectivity is required.
3. `p<z<w` **because** `ξ<m<r` and `y` is strictly increasing.
4. `ρ=F₂/q³>0` and `κ=1+ρ>1` **because** PO-0011/0012 provide strictly
   positive numerator and denominator.
5. Triangular formulas **because** two integrations of `κ=2-Q''` retain all
   endpoint terms.
6. `Λ,δ>0` **because** their integrands are positive on positive-length
   intervals and denominators `L,R,L²` are positive.
7. The translation vector field **because** the chain rule gives
   `dy(t+a)/da=q(t+a)=Q(y(t+a))` at each endpoint.
8. The sign bridge **because** differentiation of `Ψ=Λ+T log Λ`, including the
   derivative of the vector field coefficient `Q(p)`, gives the exact `N`.

## Audit edge

Commutation of simultaneous translation and `∂ξ` is not literal commutation
of constant vector fields after the coordinate change; the derivative of
`Q(p)` must be present. `PF4.CoordinateSignBridge` reconstructs the endpoint
translation objects and differentiates the actual `Psi`, so PO-0026/0027 do
not accept the printed formula as a premise.
