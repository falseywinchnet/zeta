# PO-0010 — legal ordered chord objects

Frame: S02–S04

Statement: for every `a<b`, the paper chord
`A(a,b)=s(a)-s(b)` is strictly positive and equals `integral_a^b q`; hence the
denominator in `M(a,b)=(q(b)-q(a))/A(a,b)` is nonzero and the displayed
quotient is legal.

Formalization: `PF4.PaperObjectClosure.actualAM_wellDefined_on_ordered` proves
the positivity, nonzero denominator, quotient identity, and fundamental-
theorem-of-calculus provenance in one actual-kernel statement.

Counterexample condition: ordered points with `A<=0`, a zero denominator, a
failure of the displayed quotient, or a failure of the curvature-mass
identity.

Status: `FORMALLY_PROVED`.
