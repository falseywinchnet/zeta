# PO-0032 — strict right mass of ν

Frame: S09

Statement: Under PO-0031,

\[
\nu((z,w])=\frac1{R\Lambda}\int_z^w(w-t)\kappa(t)dt>0.
\]

Proof: `RΛ>0`. The numerator integrand is continuous and strictly positive for
every `t∈(z,w)`, a nonempty interval because `z<w`. Its integral is therefore
strictly positive. The endpoints have zero Lebesgue mass, so the same formula
holds for `(z,w]`.

Non-vacuity witness: `t=(z+w)/2` lies in `(z,w)` and gives
`(w-t)κ(t)>0`.

Counterexample condition: `ν((z,w])≤0`.

Status: `CONVENTIONALLY_PROVED`.
