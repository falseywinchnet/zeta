# PO-0039 — compact-support CDF integration by parts

Frame: S09

Statement: if `A₀'=D` on `[p,w]`, then for the concrete measures

\[
\mathbb E_\nu[A_0]-\mathbb E_\mu[A_0]
=\int_p^w (F_\mu(t)-F_\nu(t))D(t)\,dt.
\]

Required proof: establish the absolutely-continuous CDF formulas, apply
integration by parts to both measures with all boundary terms retained, use
mass one and endpoint CDF values, and subtract. The equality may not be assumed
as the definition of `K` or of the transport integral.

Counterexample condition: a missing boundary term, a failure of integrability,
or a concrete input for which the two displayed sides differ.

Formalization: `PF4.Expectation.expectation_difference_eq_cdfGap_integral`
uses the independently constructed measures, their actual Bochner integrals,
their actual CDFs, and the exact interval-mass hypotheses. It proves the two
integration-by-parts formulas with the internal `z` terms retained, cancels
the `ν` terms, transports the `μ` endpoint with the fundamental theorem of
calculus, and only then rewrites the result as the CDF-gap integral.

Status: `FORMALLY_PROVED`.
