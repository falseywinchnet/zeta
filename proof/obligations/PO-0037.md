# PO-0037 — strict CDF gap

Frame: S09

Statement: For the actual probability measures of PO-0030 and PO-0031,

\[
\Delta(p)=\Delta(w)=0,
\qquad
\Delta(t)>0\quad(p<t<w).
\]

Proof:

1. Both measures are supported in `[p,w]`, are absolutely continuous (hence
   have no endpoint atoms), and have mass one. For the standard CDF
   `F(t)=μ((−∞,t])`, both CDFs are zero at `p` and one at `w`; therefore the gap
   is zero at both endpoints.
2. For `p<t≤t_*`, PO-0036 gives a strictly positive density difference on the
   nonempty interval `(p,t)`, so
   `Δ(t)=∫_p^t(fμ-fν)>0`.
3. PO-0032 gives

   \[
   \Delta(z)=1-F_\nu(z)=\nu((z,w])>0.
   \]

4. For `t_*≤t<z`, PO-0036 gives `Δ'<0`; hence

   \[
   \Delta(t)=\Delta(z)-\int_t^z\Delta'(u)du>\Delta(z)>0.
   \]

5. For `z≤t<w`, `Fμ(t)=1` and

   \[
   \Delta(t)=1-F_\nu(t)=\nu((t,w])>0,
   \]

   because the right density is strictly positive on `(t,w)`.

The intervals in Steps 2, 4, and 5 cover `(p,w)`. The proof uses strict right
mass from the actual density; it never introduces an assumed-positive endpoint
symbol.

Counterexample condition: an interior `t` with `Δ(t)≤0`, or a nonzero endpoint
gap.

Formalization: `PF4.CDF` constructs the CDFs from the actual measures and
checks strict positivity in the three regions. In the middle region the proof
is exactly “actual right-tail integral + nonnegative reverse-density
integral”; no assumed-positive endpoint symbol occurs. Endpoint and unified
case assembly remain.

Status: `FORMAL_FRAGMENT`.
