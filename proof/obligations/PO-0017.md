# PO-0017 — quotient algebra through order four

Frame: S05

Statement: For four normalized collocation rows, exact row scaling and
successive forward-difference reductions extract the first-column factors and
reduce the order-four determinant through order three and order two to the
terminal quotient difference, with the displayed forward orientation.

Quantifiers and domains: real scalar row entries and quotient coordinates;
fixed sizes two, three, and four.

Hypotheses: none for the polynomial determinant identities. Positivity is used
only in separate sign corollaries.

Formal proof:

- `PF4.GenericQuotientIntegral.factoredDet4_eq`;
- `normalizedDet4_eq_forwardDiffDet3`;
- `factoredDet3_eq`;
- `normalizedDet3_eq_forwardDiffDet2`;
- `factoredDet2_eq`;
- `quotientChainDet4_eq_terminalProduct`.

These declarations are maintained in
`proof/formal/PF4/QuotientAlgebra.lean` and compile as part of the `PF4`
library.

Non-vacuity witness: the equalities have no sign hypotheses. The strictness
corollary names every positive factor separately and does not assume the
determinant sign.

Counterexample condition: real inputs for which a displayed determinant is
not equal to its stated extracted-factor expression.

Coverage: exact discrete determinant algebra at the sizes required for PF4.
`PF4.TranslationQuotientSigns` checks the first- and second-level differential
quotient identities. `PF4.TranslationQuotientPsi` checks the terminal
logarithmic-rate identity and proves its endpoint object equals the maintained
coordinate `Psi` under the explicit coordinate realization.

Status: `FORMAL_FRAGMENT`.
