# PO-0034 — strict ratio decrease and endpoint limits

Frame: S09

Statement: Define on `(p,z)`

\[
r(t)=\frac{\Lambda(z-t)}{L\delta(t-p)}.
\]

Then `r` is strictly decreasing,

\[
r'(t)=-\frac{\Lambda(z-p)}{L\delta(t-p)^2}<0,
\]

and `r(t)→+∞` as `t→p+`, while `r(t)→0` as `t→z-`.

Proof: Quotient differentiation yields the displayed identity because

\[
-(t-p)-(z-t)=-(z-p).
\]

Every factor in the denominator is positive and the numerator
`Λ(z-p)` is positive, so the derivative is strictly negative. At `p+`, the
numerator tends to `Λ(z-p)>0` while the positive denominator tends to zero. At
`z-`, the numerator tends to zero while the denominator tends to
`Lδ(z-p)>0`.

Counterexample condition: a nonnegative derivative or failure of either
one-sided limit.

Status: `CONVENTIONALLY_PROVED`.
