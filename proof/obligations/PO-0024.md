# PO-0024 — derivative identity and positive triangular formula for delta

Frame: S06

Statement: for `p<z`, define `Lambda` by PO-0023 with `z,w` fixed and

\[
\delta=\frac{(z-p)+Q'(p)-Q[p,z]}{z-p}.
\]

Then `partial_p Lambda=-delta` and

\[
\delta=\frac1{(z-p)^2}\int_p^z(z-t)(2-Q''(t))\,dt>0
\]

whenever `2-Q''` is continuous and strictly positive on `[p,z]`.

Proof: the quotient rule gives the endpoint derivative of the same
`coordinateLambda` object. An explicit decreasing-weight primitive gives the
integral identity. The integrand is nonnegative on `[p,z]` and strictly
positive at `(p+z)/2`; strict integral positivity and `(z-p)^2>0` give the
claim.

Non-vacuity witness: `Q(t)=t`, `Q1(t)=1`, `Q2(t)=0` satisfies the jet and
positive-curvature hypotheses; generally `(p+z)/2` lies in the nonempty open
interval and has strictly positive weighted curvature.

Counterexample condition: a permitted coordinate jet for which the endpoint
derivative, integral representation, or strict sign fails.

Formalization: `PF4.Curvature.hasDerivAt_coordinateLambda_left`,
`coordinateDelta_eq_triangular`, and `coordinateDelta_pos`.

Status: `FORMALLY_PROVED`.
