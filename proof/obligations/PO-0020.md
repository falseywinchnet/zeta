# PO-0020 — strict quotient sign transfer and PF4 equivalence

Frame: S05

Statement: Under the proved lower-order quotient signs, weak decrease of
`Ψ(·;m,r)` is equivalent to global PF4, and strict decrease implies every
strictly ordered order-four translation minor is strictly positive.

Quantifiers and domains: every admissible ordered translate tuple and every
strictly ordered row-node tuple; sufficiently smooth positive kernel.

Formal fragment:

`PF4.ContinuousQuotientBox.rawFactoredDet4_pos_of_full_quotient_chain`
proves the complete generic strict local-to-global direction at fixed order
four. It derives positivity of the original unnormalized minor from:

1. strict row-node order;
2. positive first-column values;
3. exact quotient derivative identities;
4. pointwise positivity of the three successive quotient derivatives.

The proof contains exact `4→3` triple-integral and `3→2` double-integral
identities and a terminal fundamental-theorem step. It does not assume the
sign of any determinant, finite difference, or integral.

Unresolved instance bridge:

- instantiate the maintained first two quotient sign conversions with the
  actual Riemann-kernel `q>0` and `Lambda>0` proofs;
- convert maintained `∂ξΨ<0` to positivity of `(w₄/w₃)'` with the exact
  `p₄<p₃` orientation;
- formalize the converse via one-sided confluence for the full equivalence.

The actual translate functions, their derivative/factor identities, the
`rawFactoredDet4`/`PF4.translationMinor` identity, and the conditional first
two quotient conversions are maintained Lean theorems.

Non-vacuity witness: all three strict node separations are consumed by strict
interval-integral positivity. Zero-length boxes cannot close the theorem.

Counterexample condition: an admissible instantiated kernel satisfying every
named quotient identity and strict derivative sign but having a nonpositive
strictly ordered order-four minor.

Status: `FORMAL_FRAGMENT`.
