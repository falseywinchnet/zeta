# PO-0030 — normalization of μ

Frame: S08

Statement: Let `p<z`, `L=z-p`, let `κ` be continuous and strictly positive on
`[p,z]`, and define

\[
\delta=\frac1{L^2}\int_p^z(z-t)\kappa(t)\,dt.
\]

Then `δ>0`, and

\[
f_\mu(t)=\frac{(z-t)\kappa(t)}{L^2\delta}\mathbf1_{[p,z]}(t)
\]

is a nonnegative integrable density of total mass one.

Quantifiers and domains: real `p,z`; continuous `κ:[p,z]→ℝ`; strict
inequalities as stated.

Hypotheses: derived in S06 from `ξ<m`, strict coordinate monotonicity, and
`κ>1`.

Proof:

1. `L>0` because `p<z`.
2. `(z-t)κ(t)>0` on the nonempty open interval `(p,z)`, and is nonnegative on
   `[p,z]`.
3. Its integral is strictly positive by continuity and strict positivity on a
   nonempty open interval. Division by `L²>0` gives `δ>0`.
4. The displayed density is measurable, integrable, and nonnegative because it
   is a compactly supported continuous numerator divided by a positive
   constant.
5. Direct substitution of the definition of `δ` gives

   \[
   \int_\mathbb R f_\mu(t)dt
   =\frac{1}{L^2\delta}\int_p^z(z-t)\kappa(t)dt=1.
   \]

Non-vacuity witness: `(p+z)/2∈(p,z)` and the numerator is positive there.

Counterexample condition: negative density somewhere or total mass unequal to
one.

Formalization: `PF4.Measures.muMeasure` constructs the concrete restricted-
density measure, and `muMeasure_univ_eq_one` proves mass one from the checked
interval identity, integrability, and positivity hypotheses. The upstream Lean
derivation of `δ>0` and integrability remains.
