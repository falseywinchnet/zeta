# PO-0014 — weighted-mean identities and variation bound

Frame: S04

Statement: for the literal actual curvature `q`, the chord quotient `M(a,b)`
is the `q`-weighted mean of `f₀=q'/q`.  On every `ξ<m<r`, compact extrema
bound the two adjacent means, their difference is bounded by the integral of
`max(f₀',0)`, and consequently

\[
 \int_\xi^r \frac{\min(q^3,F_2)}{q^2}\,dt
 \leq \Lambda(\xi;m,r).
\]

The displayed integral and hence `Lambda` are strictly positive.

Quantifiers and domains: every ordered real triple `ξ<m<r`; complete compact
intervals and the full positive-length interval `[ξ,r]`.

Proof: `A=∫q` and the fundamental theorem for `q` give the weighted-mean
identity.  The extreme-value theorem supplies a left minimizer and right
maximizer.  A general positive-variation lemma follows from the fundamental
theorem, pointwise `f'≤max(f',0)`, and monotonicity under interval inclusion.
Quotient differentiation gives `f₀'=F₁/q²`; pointwise case analysis converts
the remainder exactly to `min(q³,F₂)/q²`.  The certified actual signs
`q>0,F₂>0` make its integral strict.

Formalization: `PF4.WeightedMeanVariation.actualM_eq_weightedMean`,
`actual_weightedMean_extrema`, `actual_weightedMean_difference_le_variation`,
`actual_lowerLambda_ge_integral_min`, and
`actual_weightedMeanVariation_lowerLambda`.

Non-vacuity: strict endpoint order supplies nonempty compact intervals,
positive `q`-mass, and a positive-length final integral.  No denominator or
`Lambda` sign is assumed.

Counterexample condition: an ordered actual triple for which the weighted
identity, variation bound, displayed lower bound, or strict integral sign
fails.

Status: `FORMALLY_PROVED`.
