# PO-0035 — constructive existence of the density crossing

Frame: S09

Statement: Under the preceding obligations, the point

\[
t_* = \frac{\Lambda z+L\delta p}{\Lambda+L\delta}
\]

lies strictly between `p` and `z` and satisfies `fμ(t_*)=fν(t_*)`.

Proof: Put `a=Λ>0` and `b=Lδ>0`. Then

\[
t_*=\frac{a}{a+b}z+\frac{b}{a+b}p
\]

is a strict convex combination, so `p<t_*<z`. Moreover

\[
z-t_*=\frac{b(z-p)}{a+b},\qquad
t_*-p=\frac{a(z-p)}{a+b}.
\]

Substitution into the actual ratio from PO-0033 gives

\[
r(t_*)=\frac{a}{b}\frac{b(z-p)}{a(z-p)}=1.
\]

Since `fν(t_*)>0`, ratio one is equivalent to equality of the densities.

Non-vacuity witness: the crossing is explicitly constructed; existence does
not follow from an empty or unreachable branch.

Counterexample condition: `t_*` outside `(p,z)` or unequal densities there.

Formalization: `PF4.Crossing.crossingPoint_mem` checks the strict location and
`densityRatio_at_crossingPoint` checks the ratio-one identity.
