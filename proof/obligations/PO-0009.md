# PO-0009 — global logarithmic kernel objects

Frame: S02

Statement: for the maintained global Riemann kernel, define
`ell=log Phi`, `s=ell'`, and `q=-s'`. Then `exp ell=Phi`, the two derivative
identities hold at every real point, and `q>0` globally.

Formalization:
`PF4.PaperObjectClosure.actualLogSlopeCurvature_globally_wellDefined` packages
the literal objects and all four claims. Kernel positivity justifies the real
logarithm; no zero-value branch of `Real.log` is used.

Counterexample condition: a real point where the kernel is nonpositive, the
logarithmic derivative differs from `s`, the slope derivative differs from
`-q`, or `q` is nonpositive.
