# PO-0036 — uniqueness and derivative sign pattern

Frame: S09

Statement: The point `t_*` of PO-0035 is the unique point of `(p,z)` where
`fμ=fν`. If `Δ=Fμ-Fν`, then `Δ'(t)>0` for `p<t<t_*` and `Δ'(t)<0` for
`t_*<t<z`.

Proof: PO-0034 makes the actual ratio `r=fμ/fν` strictly decreasing, so the
ratio-one point is unique. Directly clearing positive denominators also gives

\[
r(t)>1\iff t<t_*,\qquad r(t)<1\iff t>t_*.
\]

On `(p,z)` the CDFs are differentiable with derivatives equal to their
continuous densities. Since `fν(t)>0`,

\[
\Delta'(t)=f_\mu(t)-f_\nu(t)=f_\nu(t)(r(t)-1),
\]

which has the stated strict sign.

Counterexample condition: a second crossing or a derivative sign opposite the
stated interval.

Status: `CONVENTIONALLY_PROVED`.
