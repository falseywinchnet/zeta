# PO-0031 — normalization of ν

Frame: S08

Statement: Let `p<z<w`, `L=z-p`, `R=w-z`, let `κ` be continuous and strictly
positive on `[p,w]`, and define

\[
\Lambda=\frac1L\int_p^z(t-p)\kappa(t)dt+
\frac1R\int_z^w(w-t)\kappa(t)dt.
\]

Then `Λ>0`, and the two-piece function

\[
f_\nu(t)=
\frac{(t-p)\kappa(t)}{L\Lambda}\mathbf1_{[p,z]}(t)+
\frac{(w-t)\kappa(t)}{R\Lambda}\mathbf1_{[z,w]}(t)
\]

is a nonnegative integrable density of total mass one. The double definition
at the singleton `{z}` is immaterial to the induced absolutely continuous
measure; a formal implementation should use half-open pieces or define the
density piecewise once.

Proof:

1. `L,R>0` from `p<z<w`.
2. Each weighted numerator is nonnegative on its closed interval and positive
   on its interior.
3. Both weighted integrals are strictly positive, hence `Λ>0`.
4. The density is measurable, compactly supported, integrable, and
   nonnegative.
5. Ignoring the measure-zero shared endpoint,

   \[
   \int_\mathbb R f_\nu
   =\frac1\Lambda\left[
   \frac1L\int_p^z(t-p)\kappa(t)dt+
   \frac1R\int_z^w(w-t)\kappa(t)dt\right]=1.
   \]

Non-vacuity witness: the midpoints of `(p,z)` and `(z,w)` make the respective
piece numerators strictly positive.

Counterexample condition: a negative piece, zero normalizer, or mass not one.

Status: `CONVENTIONALLY_PROVED`.
