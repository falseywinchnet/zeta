# PO-0018 — iterated quotient-integral determinant identity

Frame: S05

Statement: Let `I⊆ℝ` be an interval, let `k≥2`, and let
`f₁,…,fₖ : I→ℝ` be continuously differentiable with `f₁(t)>0` on `I`. Define
`Gⱼ=fⱼ/f₁`. For `t₁<⋯<tₖ` in `I`,

\[
\det[f_j(t_i)]_{i,j=1}^{k}
=\left(\prod_{i=1}^k f_1(t_i)\right)
\int_{t_1}^{t_2}\!\cdots\!\int_{t_{k-1}}^{t_k}
\det[G'_{j+1}(\tau_i)]_{i,j=1}^{k-1}
\,d\tau_1\cdots d\tau_{k-1}.
\]

Quantifiers and domains: finite `k≥2`; real interval `I`; strictly ordered
nodes; `C¹` functions; pointwise positive first function.

Hypotheses: no Riemann-kernel assumption. Positivity of `f₁` ensures quotient
definitions and later sign transfer; nonvanishing alone suffices for the
algebraic identity.

Proof:

1. Factor `f₁(tᵢ)` from row `i`. The determinant becomes the product of those
   factors times the determinant of a matrix whose first column is one and
   whose remaining entries are `Gⱼ(tᵢ)`.
2. For `i=k-1,k-2,…,1` in descending order, replace row `i+1` by row `i+1`
   minus row `i`. Descending order is essential: each subtracted row is still
   the original normalized row. These operations preserve the determinant.
3. The first column becomes `(1,0,…,0)`. Expansion along that column leaves
   the `(k-1)×(k-1)` determinant

   \[
   \det[G_{j+1}(t_{i+1})-G_{j+1}(t_i)]_{i,j=1}^{k-1}.
   \]

4. The fundamental theorem of calculus gives each row entry as

   \[
   G_{j+1}(t_{i+1})-G_{j+1}(t_i)
   =\int_{t_i}^{t_{i+1}}G'_{j+1}(\tau_i)d\tau_i.
   \]

5. Determinant multilinearity in its finitely many rows moves the independent
   integrals outside. Equivalently, expand the determinant as a finite signed
   permutation sum and interchange that finite sum with the product integral.
   This gives the stated formula with no missing factorial or orientation sign.

Non-vacuity witness: strict node order gives every interval
`[tᵢ,tᵢ₊₁]` positive length. For sign transfer, a continuous strictly positive
reduced determinant therefore integrates to a strictly positive number.

Depends on: determinant row-scaling and row-addition identities, the
fundamental theorem of calculus, finite sum/integral interchange.

Expanded because:

- determinant reduction because of exact row operations;
- differences become integrals because of `C¹` regularity;
- determinant of row integrals becomes a product integral because determinant
  is finite multilinear algebra.

Method: conventional arbitrary-order proof; fixed sizes two through four are
maintained Lean proofs.

Lean declarations: `PF4.ContinuousQuotientBox.normalizedDet4_eq_tripleIntegral`,
`normalizedDet3_eq_doubleIntegral`, and their strict positivity corollaries in
`proof/formal/PF4/QuotientIntegral.lean`.

Trusted base: Lean 4.32.0 and mathlib 4.32.0 for fixed sizes; ordinary real
integration and determinant algebra for the remaining arbitrary-order claim.

Counterexample condition: admissible functions/nodes for which the two exact
expressions differ.

Coverage: the conventional statement covers all finite `k≥2`. Lean covers
exactly `k=2,3,4`, every strict node tuple, all orientation signs, and strict
box transfer. No sign premise on a determinant or integral is used.

Status: `FORMAL_FRAGMENT`.
