## 01 — The Thirty-Seventh Interval

Use case: photorealistic-natural
Asset type: wide cinematic background for a literary story collection
Primary request: Photograph the instant Dr. Ilyana Sero clamps a punctured radiator on the floor of Shackleton crater while a vast concentric scientific interferometer glints behind her. The image must feel physically lunar and observational, not like ornamental space art.
Scene/backdrop: Airless lunar south-pole crater at 04:20 station time; the Sun skims the horizon from camera-left at eleven degrees, making razor-hard shadows and bright semicircles on bolt heads. A fan of frozen transparent coolant feathers surrounds a small micrometeor rupture. Thirty-seven low silver superconducting rails form nested intervals across the crater floor; a sapphire enclosure stands at their center. Earth is small and low in the background.
Subject: One suited scientist at the radiator, leaning her full weight into a coarse metal clamp; scratched white EVA suit, amber palm-heat warning, raw trace spindle clipped to chest, no readable labels. Show the labor and the exact damaged manifold, not a heroic pose.
Composition/framing: 2.39:1-feeling landscape. Physically present camera 1.35 meters above regolith, 22 meters from the scientist, angled downward five degrees with crater rails leading toward the sapphire center. Scientist occupies the left-middle third; the unresolved dark horizon and nested geometry fill the rest.
Camera: Ruggedized full-frame mirrorless camera in a vacuum housing, 35 mm prime, f/8, 1/500 s, ISO 160, focus at 24 m with deep but not infinite depth, white balance 5400 K, expose for sunlit suit while retaining subtle detail in open shadows only where reflected light plausibly reaches.
Lighting/mood: Unfiltered white solar light, hard-edged and low-angle; Earthshine is extremely faint and blue. No atmospheric glow, no stars visible in exposed daylight-black sky except perhaps one faint point. Severe, precise, exultant restraint.
Materials/textures: Powdery regolith over sharp fragments, machined aluminum, scratched multilayer insulation, transparent coolant ice, sapphire, scorched clamp thread, believable EVA fabric and joints.
Constraints: Exactly one person; physically coherent lunar scale and shadows; no floating dust clouds; no magical glow; no visible text.

Asset type: wide cinematic background for a literary story collection

Scene/backdrop: Interior of a materially credible future survey ship at 18:04 ship time, seen through a broad forward blister. A rogue planet fills the lower half: nitrogen ice, black continental fracture, one tiny warm sodium-yellow lamp at the edge of a buried habitat. A matte-black tungsten furnace-seed cradle with four pale guidance petals descends toward the surface. Behind and high right, the gate is a narrow lensing oval that warps a sparse starfield without emitting fantasy light; an old amber globular cluster lies far beyond.

Use case: photorealistic-natural
Asset type: wide cinematic background for a literary story collection
Primary request: Photograph Dr. Nara Vey at the emergency cryogenic manifold one heartbeat after the isolated safety relay accepts a microscopic detector dark count, allowing her to pull the east coolant lever while fire advances through an Antarctic research station.

Cramped steel manifold recess with two valve levers, west pressure gauge visibly shattered, east lever moving through ninety degrees.
