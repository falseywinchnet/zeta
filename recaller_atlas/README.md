# Recaller Atlas

Ten fantastic stories. Thirteen photographic backgrounds. One rule: notice the
instant completely.

The Recaller supplies the pressure—the bright eyes, unsteady smoke, sweat, and
refusal to let the small or failed disappear. The Director supplies the camera:
where it stands, what light reaches it, what the materials can bear, and what
the sensor is allowed to make vivid without falsifying the scene.

All stories are fiction. Repository mathematics and model interviews supplied
motifs of memory, thresholds, failed routes, exact support, and the passage from
maps to mechanisms. No fictional event is a mathematical or historical claim.
No Twitter or Substack post was consulted.

## Background collection

- [`GALLERY.jpg`](GALLERY.jpg) is a 1920×1080 contact sheet in reading order:
  four central frames, three Ash Archivist frames, three Event-Horizon Witness
  frames, and three Midwife frames.
- The central story has four frames in [`images/`](images/): the Recaller at
  the flywheel, the blackout moth, the unfired thumbprint, and the survey robot
  interrupted by a snail.
- Each specialist story has one matching frame in its contributor `images/`
  directory.
- Production prompts are preserved in the central
  [`image-prompts.md`](image-prompts.md) and each contributor’s
  `image-prompts.md`.
- The shared finish is recorded in [`DIRECTOR_PLATE.md`](DIRECTOR_PLATE.md).
- All accepted finals are PNG landscapes. The three ultra-wide lunar/cosmic
  frames are 1814–1935 pixels wide; the remaining frames are 1535–1672 pixels
  wide.

The built-in image generator produced every frame. Every final was inspected
at original resolution. Six candidates were rejected or superseded for a
story-critical mismatch: the potter’s contact finger, the tardigrade’s anatomy,
the archive moth’s species/material, the snail’s crossing geometry, the first
Orion scene’s emissive/ambient exterior, and its next drone-like furnace seed.
The independent gate is preserved in [`qa/`](qa/); `director_third_cut.md`
clears the final thirteen-frame collection.
