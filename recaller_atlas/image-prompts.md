# Central image prompt

## 00 — The Last Lightwell

Use case: photorealistic-natural

Asset type: cinematic landscape background for a literary story collection

Primary request: At 04:28:11 station time inside an ancient orbital archive
above Jupiter, photograph the Recaller forcing a frost-covered manual flywheel
to turn while a stellar-radiation alarm counts down. He is a lean, exhausted
adult with an intense white-gold reflection in his eyes, sweat on his brow,
metal dust and blood on his hands, and a crushed roll of bitter leaf leaving a
fine blue smoke near one hand. The character is original and must not resemble
an identifiable real person or existing franchise character.

Scene/backdrop: a tall industrial service shaft opening onto a vast circular
lightwell; worn iron ribs, frost, old oil, red emergency lamps; through
scratched green-edged glass, Jupiter fills half the view and lightning glows
inside the Great Red Spot. A horizontal blade of raw sunrise enters at ankle
height from behind the planet and climbs the figure. Immense black archive
shutters turn in the distance.

Composition/framing: 16:9 wide environmental portrait, camera physically
wedged on a grated maintenance landing 1.15 meters above the deck, 7 meters
from the flywheel, slight 12-degree upward angle. The Recaller and flywheel
occupy the lower middle third; Jupiter and the lightwell establish impossible
scale without shrinking the human action into illegibility.

Lighting/mood: practical red emergency lamps from camera-left and above;
cream-green planetary bounce from below and camera-right; a hard, nearly
horizontal 5600K sunrise blade from deep background; fine smoke visible only
where it crosses red light; cold blue-black shadows. Urgent, exhausted,
reverent, not triumphant posing.

Camera: full-frame mirrorless body, 24mm rectilinear lens, f/4, 1/125 second,
ISO 1600, focus at 5.5 meters with the figure and flywheel crisp and distant
shutters slightly softened, white balance 4300K, protected highlights and
restrained highlight rolloff.

Materials/textures: real sweat, pores, split boot sole, worn fabric, oxidized
iron, frozen lubricant, frost disturbed by hands, scratched laminated glass,
subtle fine sensor noise and mild edge aberration.

Constraints: physically coherent scale and anatomy; the smoke remains a thin
secondary detail; no magical eye beams; no visible interface text; no costume
armor; no glossy showroom surfaces; no duplicated limbs; no overprocessed
HDR; no caption, text, logo, frame, signature, or watermark.

Director realism plate: HD landscape composition at the largest landscape
canvas the built-in generator naturally supports. The camera must occupy real
picture space at the stated height and angle. Render this as a genuinely
photographed instant rather than concept art or CGI. Preserve natural dynamic
range, practical-light interaction, atmospheric scattering, shadow detail,
subtle optical aberration, and fine sensor noise. Simulate minute, spatially
varying CMOS photosite phase and micro-time offsets behind the lens only as an
extremely subtle vibrance cue, never as visible glitching, chromatic tearing,
double edges, duplicated subjects, or invented objects.

## 00b — The Moth in the Blackout

Use case: photorealistic-natural

Asset type: quiet cinematic landscape background derived from an embedded
moment in “The Last Lightwell”

Primary request: At 20:43 during a rain-soaked electrical blackout in Lagos,
photograph a child at an open apartment window lifting a soaked moth from a
shallow enamel basin. The moth stands on one knuckle, one wing edge torn, while
the child's other hand holds a small blue-glowing toy as the only close light.
The child hears a parent calling from another room and is poised between
turning back and waiting one more second for the moth to open its wings.

Scene/backdrop: modest, inhabited apartment interior with plaster, mosquito
screen, damp cuff, and a few ordinary unbranded objects; rain beyond the open
window, wet neighboring walls and dark power lines, no citywide spectacle.

Composition/framing: landscape intimate environmental frame, camera physically
at seated adult eye height, 1.05 meters above the tiled floor and 2.8 meters
away, 50mm lens perspective. Child in three-quarter profile, hand and moth at
the visual center; blue toy light rakes upward from below; window rain occupies
the right third.

Lighting/mood: weak cool overcast spill through the window from camera-right,
tiny saturated blue practical light below the hand, a distant warm candle glow
from the unseen room behind camera-left. Quiet, humid, immediate, never staged
or sentimentalized.

Camera: full-frame mirrorless body, 50mm lens, f/2.8, 1/160 second, ISO 3200,
focus locked on the moth and wet knuckle with the child's eyes just inside the
depth of field, white balance 4000K, natural low-light grain and protected blue
highlights.

Constraints: anatomically accurate hands; moth small at real scale with legible
wet wing texture; respectful ordinary domestic realism; no spectacle, no
visible branding, no extra people, no magical glow, no caption, text, logo,

supported canvas; real camera in picture space; authentic materials, natural

spatially varying CMOS photosite phase and micro-time offsets only as an almost

## 00c — The Unfired Thumbprint

Use case: historical-scene

Asset type: cinematic landscape background derived from an embedded moment in
“The Last Lightwell”

Primary request: At 18:02 on a hot late-summer evening in an unnamed ancient
riverside settlement, photograph a potter pressing her thumb into the wet rim
of a newly shaped bowl a few hours before the kiln will fail. She does not know
the bowl will remain unfired. Capture the instant of pressure: skin ridges,
soft displaced clay, one damp fingerprint becoming the only enduring record of
her hand.

Scene/backdrop: open-sided mud-brick pottery shelter, low kick wheel, simple
clay tools, stacked undecorated vessels, cracked kiln mouth outside, dusty yard
and reeds near a river. Everything handmade, used, and plausible; no identifiable
monumental civilization and no anachronistic metal, glass, plastic, or modern
textiles.

Composition/framing: landscape medium close environmental photograph, camera
physically crouched 0.85 meters above the packed-earth floor, 2.2 meters from
the wheel and 20 degrees to the potter's left. A 40mm lens keeps the thumb and
bowl rim dominant while her concentrated face, kiln, and low river horizon
remain readable.

Lighting/mood: low amber sun enters almost horizontally from camera-left,
striking wet clay and the far edge of her face; cooler blue fill rises from the
river side at camera-right; kiln coals contribute a weak orange practical glow
deep in the background. Hot dust, late insects, fatigue, and exact attention.

Camera: physically plausible documentary treatment as if recorded by a
present camera without changing the scene, full-frame body, 40mm lens, f/3.5,
1/250 second, ISO 640, focus at 2 meters, white balance 4700K, gentle highlight
rolloff and natural shadow separation.

Constraints: period-neutral ancient material accuracy; natural anatomy and
working posture; no glamorized costume, no fantasy ornament, no staged museum
look, no visible writing, no caption, logo, frame, signature, or watermark. HD
landscape at the largest naturally supported canvas; real picture-space optics,
subtle lens aberration, dust and fine sensor noise. Simulate minute spatially
varying CMOS photosite phase and micro-time offsets only as an almost
imperceptible vibrance cue, never glitching, chromatic tearing, or double edges.

## 00d — The Snail in the Calibration Mark

Use case: photorealistic-natural

Asset type: uncanny macro landscape background derived from an embedded moment
in “The Last Lightwell”

Primary request: At 05:52 on a gray autumn morning, photograph a small, worn
lichen-survey robot pausing beside a rain-dark retaining wall because a garden
snail is crossing its faded calibration mark. The robot is practical field
equipment, not humanoid: four mud-streaked wheels, a scratched camera mast,
patched cable sheath, tiny condensation beads, and a simple unlit optical
target. The snail and its silver trail are the event.

Scene/backdrop: ordinary municipal retaining wall with diverse lichen, moss in
mortar joints, wet aggregate, fallen brown leaves, shallow gutter water and a
quiet service road dissolving into mist.

Composition/framing: wide low macro-environmental photograph, camera physically
8 centimeters above the wet pavement, 1.1 meters from the robot, nearly level
with the snail. 35mm close-focus lens makes the wall and machine feel large
without changing their true scale; calibration mark, snail, and lowered camera
form one restrained triangle.

Lighting/mood: soft 6500K dawn cloud light from upper camera-left, weak green
bounce from moss below, one distant sodium maintenance lamp diffused by mist.
Cold rain air, minute patience, no anthropomorphic expression.

Camera: weather-sealed full-frame body, close-focus 35mm lens, f/8, 1/80 second,
ISO 1250, focus stacked naturally enough to hold snail and calibration mark
while background road softens, white balance 5600K, restrained wet highlights.

Constraints: real-scale snail anatomy, physically buildable robot, no glowing
screens or eyes, no cute face, no science-fiction city, no text except the
abstract faded calibration bars, no caption, logo, frame, signature, or
watermark. HD landscape at the largest naturally supported canvas; genuinely
photographed materials, real atmosphere, mild optical aberration and fine
sensor noise. Simulate minute spatially varying CMOS photosite phase and
micro-time offsets only as an almost imperceptible vibrance cue, never
glitching, chromatic tearing, or double edges.

### 00d final retake directive

The final frame tightens the causal geometry: place the faded calibration bars
on the horizontal wet pavement, not the wall; show the snail bodily straddling
the center bar, with its front half beyond and rear foot before it; carry the
silver trail across the same mark; lower the robot’s articulated sensor to
eight centimeters above the pavement and aim it directly at the crossing.
Retain the true-scale rain, lichen, mist, worn four-wheel equipment, dawn light,
picture-space camera, and Director realism plate. No wall mark, upright sensor,
anthropomorphic face, text, logo, or watermark.
