# The Director's realism plate

This plate closes every production prompt in the atlas. Story-specific facts
come first; the plate controls photographic embodiment rather than inventing
content.

## Required finish

- HD landscape composition at the largest landscape canvas the built-in image
  generator naturally supports.
- A camera physically occupies the scene. State its height, angle, distance,
  and obstruction or footing when relevant.
- State a plausible camera or scientific-imaging body, lens and focal length,
  aperture, shutter speed, ISO, focus behavior, white balance, and exposure
  character.
- Name the practical sources of light, their direction, softness, color, and
  interaction with atmosphere and surfaces.
- Preserve real texture: pores, fatigue, fabric wear, dust, condensation,
  scratches, oxidation, grain, imperfect joins, and the actual limits of the
  scene's materials.
- Use restrained highlight rolloff, legible shadow detail, subtle optical
  aberration, and fine sensor noise. Avoid overprocessed HDR.
- Render a genuinely photographed instant in real picture space, never generic
  concept art, a glossy 3-D render, or a stage tableau.
- Simulate minute, spatially varying phase and micro-time offsets among CMOS
  photosites behind the lens only as an extremely subtle vibrance cue. They
  must never become visible glitching, chromatic tearing, double edges,
  duplicated subjects, or invented objects.
- No caption, text, logo, frame, signature, or watermark.

The photosite instruction is an aesthetic simulation, not a claim about a
physical camera process.
